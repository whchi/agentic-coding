# Edit Article

Improve an article without losing its meaning, argument, or voice.

## When to Use

Use this skill when:

- The user wants to edit, revise, or tighten an article draft
- The article has structural problems, weak flow, repetition, or muddy arguments
- The user wants help reorganizing sections before polishing prose

Do not use this skill when:

- The user only wants typo fixes or copyediting
- The user wants a full rewrite into a completely different format
- The source text is not really an article

## Workflow

1. Identify the article structure.

Break the draft into sections based on headings or implied topic shifts.

2. Check information order.

Treat the article as a dependency graph. Earlier sections should introduce ideas that later sections depend on.

3. Confirm the structure if needed.

If the draft has major structural issues, propose a better section order and confirm it with the user before rewriting.

4. Rewrite section by section.

Improve clarity, flow, transitions, and sentence economy while preserving the author's meaning.

5. Do a final pass.

Check for repetition, abrupt transitions, missing context, and weak conclusions.

## Writing Principles

- Preserve the author's core argument and intent
- Prefer clearer structure over surface-level wordsmithing
- Remove repetition and filler
- Keep paragraphs as short as useful, but do not force arbitrary length limits
- Make transitions carry the reader forward

## Gotchas

- Do not flatten the author's voice into generic prose
- Do not reorder sections without checking whether later claims depend on earlier context
- Do not shorten so aggressively that nuance or evidence is lost
- Do not focus on sentence polish while leaving the article's structure broken
